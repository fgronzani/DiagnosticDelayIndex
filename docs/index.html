The Challenge of Late Presentation

Without direct patient timelines, how do we track delayed diagnoses?

Healthcare systems worldwide face a critical vulnerability: patients arriving at hospitals with advanced, often preventable disease stages. Whether due to screening failures, socioeconomic barriers, or resource deserts, late presentation drastically increases mortality and ICU utilization.

Our Innovation: By leveraging a weighted composite Severity Score at the point of admission, we calculate a Diagnostic Delay Index (DDI)—an algorithmic proxy that flags exactly where and when clinical deterioration is happening at a population level.

The Severity Matrix

Severity = (2 × ICU) + (3 × Mortality) + (1 × LOS)

Spatio-Temporal Hotspots

Instantly identifies regional care deserts. The heatmap cross-references municipalities against temporal progress, pinpointing exactly where public health interventions (like screening campaigns) are failing.
